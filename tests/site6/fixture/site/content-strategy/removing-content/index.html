Removing content

Remove unwanted, redundant and out-of-date content to help users find what they need.

Created by
Digital Transformation Agency and National Archives of Australia
Reviewed on
13 September 2017

Help for government agencies to know how to remove unwanted, redundant and out-of-date content.

Why remove content?

We know that there are over 40 million GOV.AU web pages across the internet today. So, it’s no wonder users get confused when they interact with government. It’s time that we started cleaning up our digital estate.

There are 4 steps to removing your content from the web.

How to remove content

  • Step 1: Get approval to remove
    You will need to get buy-in from the content owner before you can take anything off your website.
  • Step 2: Create a removal plan
    Both internal and external people need this information.
  • Step 3: Archive and record content
    Keep and record all government content for future access.
  • Step 4: Remove content from the web
    The steps you need to consider to responsibly remove content.
