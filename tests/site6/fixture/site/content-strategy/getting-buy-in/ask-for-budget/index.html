Ask for budget

Most business owners think in terms of return on investment. You should show the value of your content improvement in numbers if you can.

Contents

  • Prove the value
  • Talk about risk
  • When to ask for budget

Prove the value

It is useful to benchmark your current online services so that you can show the cost of poor content. Measurements can include:

  • costs of transactions over the phone or in person
  • online completion rates
  • digital take-up
  • user satisfaction

A benchmark will help you measure performance as you improve content. It will also help you show the return on investment from good content.

A content audit can help prove your case as it will show what you have and what needs to be done. You will have to prove the value of a content audit first.

Try to give your stakeholders options, such as a sample content audit versus a full website content audit.

Talk about risk

There will always be an element of risk. You need to talk about risk, but compare it to the overall benefit. This will highlight how much you can save through content improvement in the long term.

Think about how your stakeholders feel about risk and how open they are to change the workflow process.

When to ask for budget

Work out when is the best time to ask for budget. This may be at the start of the financial year when budgets are being allocated, or at the end when teams may have spare budget.
